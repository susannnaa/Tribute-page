Lana Del Rey

Not just a women

Elizabeth Woolridge Grant (born June 21, 1985), known professionally as Lana Del Rey

Here's a time line of Lana Del Rey's life

  • 1985 - Born as Elizabeth Woolridge Grant in New York City, New York
  • 2004 - Begins performing in clubs in New York City under the name Lizzy Grant, but later changes her stage name to Lana Del Rey
  • 2008 - Releases her debut album "Sirens" under the name Lana Del Ray (later re-released as "Lana Del Rey A.K.A. Lizzy Grant")
  • 2011 - Gains widespread attention and popularity with the viral success of her single "Video Games"
  • 2012 - Releases her second studio album "Born to Die," which becomes a commercial success and solidifies her position as a unique pop icon
  • 2014 - Releases her third studio album "Ultraviolence," receiving praise for its darker and more introspective themes
  • 2015 - Releases "Honeymoon," her fourth studio album, which continues to showcase her signature cinematic and vintage-inspired sound
  • 2017 - Drops her fifth studio album "Lust for Life," featuring collaborations with prominent artists and a more positive tone
  • 2020 - Releases her sixth studio album "Chemtrails over the Country Club," exploring Americana and folk influences
  • 2021 - Releases her seventh studio album "Blue Banisters," showcasing a more stripped-down and intimate musical style
  • 2023 - Continues to evolve her musical and artistic style, cementing her status as a prominent and influential figure in the music industry
